Our Conversion Capabilities

Sheeting

We convert parent reels into flat sheets at our dedicated sheeting line. Reels are cut to your specified sheet length and stacked on pallets ready for dispatch. Suitable for a wide range of substrates including folding boxboard, kraft papers and speciality grades, in large-format sizes to your exact requirement.

Slitting

Our slitting line takes wide parent reels and slits them down to the narrower widths your machine needs. Whether you need a single width for a dedicated line or multiple widths from the same reel, we match the job to your running format.

Rewinding

We rewind reels to new core sizes, different outer diameters or tidied-up finished rolls from part-used stock. Rewinding is often used to prepare material for a specific press, consolidate part-reels, or refresh damaged or mislabelled stock.

Guillotining

Our industrial guillotine handles large-format board and stacks of sheets where precise trimming is needed. It suits non-standard sheet sizes, trimmed blanks, sample batches, point-of-sale work and prototype runs — any time a standard sheet size doesn't quite fit.

Rewrapping

We rewrap reels, pallets and part-loads to the standards your production line needs, whether that's damage-protected wrapping for long-distance transit, repackaging for onward distribution, or bringing mill stock up to spec before it reaches your floor.

Export Packing

For goods heading overseas, we prepare shipments to the packing standards international transit demands. Export packing covers secure wrapping, labelling and load preparation, so material arrives at its destination in the condition it left ours.

Pallet Turning

Sometimes material just needs to be moved from one pallet to another — different base, different orientation, different stack configuration, or to split a full pallet into smaller units for onward distribution. We handle pallet turning as a standalone service or as part of a wider conversion job.

Sustainability Through Reuse and Reduced Waste

Conversion isn't just about making material fit — it's one of the most practical ways to reduce waste in a paper and board supply chain. Cutting to size on site means customers only receive what they can use, so less material ends up as offcut.

Reworking Redundant Stock

When stock is no longer needed for its original order, we rework it wherever we can — guillotining to new sizes, rewinding to different widths, or repackaging for a new customer. Material that would otherwise be written off gets a second life.

Converting to Size

By sheeting, slitting and cutting to your exact specification, we help customers avoid the offcuts and trim waste that come with buying standard sizes and trimming down in-house.

Reducing Waste Across the Operation

Careful handling, controlled storage and efficient planning keep damage, mis-cuts and scrap to a minimum. What waste we do generate is sorted and sent for recycling.

Trade Services

We don't just convert our own stock. Manufacturers, printers and converters regularly send us their own materials for processing — whether it's converting parent reels to press-ready sizes, reworking surplus stock, or repacking material that's been damaged in transit.

Customer-Owned Material

Send us your own reels, sheets or pallets and we'll convert them to the sizes and formats you need. This is a practical option when you've bought direct from a mill but need the material sheeted, slit, rewound or repacked before it reaches your production line.

Reworking Damaged or Surplus Stock

Stock that's been damaged, mislabelled or left over from a cancelled order doesn't have to be written off. We can guillotine it to new sizes, rewind damaged reels, strip and repack pallets, or reformat material so it can be used elsewhere or resold.

Rewinding, Sheeting & Repacking

If your own equipment can't handle a particular job — or you simply don't have the capacity — we can step in. We run sheeting, slitting, rewinding and repacking as a standalone trade service, with material returned to you or delivered direct to your customer.

Quality Assurance

Quality is embedded at every stage of the conversion process. All incoming reels and sheets are checked against mill test certificates before processing begins. During conversion, our operators monitor dimensions, edge quality and surface condition continuously. Finished goods are inspected and recorded against your purchase order before palletising and wrapping.

Scalderhurst holds both FSC and PEFC chain-of-custody certification, providing independently verified traceability from the forest to your factory. Every delivery is accompanied by the relevant certification documentation, ensuring your supply chain meets responsible sourcing requirements for retail, food packaging and export markets.
