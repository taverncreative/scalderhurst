Resources & Guides

Drawing on over fifty years of experience in the paper and board industry, we have compiled a collection of technical resources to help you make informed decisions about materials, grades and compliance requirements.

Technical Resources

Paper Weight Conversion Guide

Paper weight is expressed differently depending on the region and standard in use. Our conversion guide explains the relationship between grams per square metre (gsm), US basis weights and imperial measurements, so you can compare specifications across international suppliers with confidence. Whether you are sourcing 80 gsm office stock or 350 gsm packaging board, this guide makes it straightforward to find the equivalent weight in any system.

Board Grade Specifications

Choosing the correct board grade is essential for product protection, print quality and cost efficiency. This resource provides an overview of the most commonly used grades in the UK market, including solid bleached board (SBB), folding boxboard (FBB), white-lined chipboard (WLC) and corrugated fluting. Each grade profile covers typical caliper ranges, burst strength, printability and recommended end uses.

Sustainability & Certification

Responsible sourcing is central to how we operate at Scalderhurst. This guide outlines the key certification schemes relevant to the paper and board sector, including FSC (Forest Stewardship Council) and PEFC (Programme for the Endorsement of Forest Certification). It also covers our own environmental commitments, from chain-of-custody certification to waste reduction targets across our Kent-based warehousing and distribution operations.

Packaging Regulations Overview

Businesses that use paper and board for food contact, retail packaging or transit protection must comply with a range of UK and European regulations. This overview covers the essentials, including food contact material requirements under EC 1935/2004, the EU Timber Regulation (EUTR), REACH obligations and Extended Producer Responsibility (EPR) rules. It is designed to give procurement and packaging teams a clear starting point for understanding their compliance responsibilities.
